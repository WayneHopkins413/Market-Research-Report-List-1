<p><p>Electrocoating, or E-coat, is a surface finishing process that uses electrical current to deposit paint onto metallic substrates. The market is primarily divided into cathodic and anodic types. Cathodic electrocoating, commonly used for its corrosion resistance and even coverage, involves negatively charged particles depositing on a positive substrate. Anodic electrocoating, while less common, utilizes positively charged particles and offers distinct aesthetic finishes. Both types find applications in automotive, industrial, and consumer goods sectors, emphasizing durability and quality.</p></p>
