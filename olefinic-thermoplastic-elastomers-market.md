<p><p>The Olefinic Thermoplastic Elastomers market primarily comprises two types: Thermoplastic Polyolefin (TPO) and Thermoplastic Vulcanizates (TPV). TPOs are blends of polypropylene and elastomers, offering excellent flexibility, chemical resistance, and lightweight properties, making them popular in automotive and consumer goods. Conversely, TPVs are cross-linked rubber particles dispersed in a polyolefin matrix, combining the elasticity of rubber with the processing ease of thermoplastics. They are widely used for applications requiring enhanced durability and thermal stability, such as seals and gaskets.</p></p>
