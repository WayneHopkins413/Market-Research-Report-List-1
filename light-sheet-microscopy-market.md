<p><p>Light Sheet Microscopy (LSM) encompasses various types, notably Selective Plane Illumination Microscopy (SPIM) and Dual-View Selective Plane Illumination Microscopy (DiSPIM). SPIM uses a thin sheet of light to illuminate samples, allowing for high-speed imaging with minimal photodamage, making it ideal for live organism studies. DiSPIM enhances this by using two detectors for improved spatial resolution and reduced glare. Other emerging techniques in the market may include variants that further optimize imaging or adapt to different sample types, broadening LSM applications.</p></p>
