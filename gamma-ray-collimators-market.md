There are two primary types: panoramic collimators, which provide a wide field of view and are ideal for whole-body imaging, enabling effective detection of radiation sources across large areas; and directional collimators, designed for focused measurements, allowing precise targeting of specific sources. Each type serves distinct needs in medical diagnostics and nuclear safety, optimizing the accuracy of gamma ray detection.</p></p>
