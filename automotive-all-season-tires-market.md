<p><p>The automotive all-season tire market is primarily divided into two types: bias tires and radial tires. Bias tires have plies that run diagonally, providing a sturdy build but less flexibility, often resulting in reduced tread life and traction. In contrast, radial tires feature plies that are arranged at a right angle to the tire's direction, enhancing flexibility, fuel efficiency, and handling. The radial design has become predominant due to better performance in varying weather conditions, making it favored for all-season applications.</p></p>
